Main regions and their functions

Frontal Lobe

The frontal lobe controls thinking, decision making, problem-solving, emotions, and voluntary movement. It plays a major role in personality and behavior.

Parietal Lobe

The parietal lobe processes sensory information such as touch, temperature, and pain. It helps us understand spatial awareness and body position.

Temporal Lobe

The temporal lobe is responsible for hearing, memory, and understanding language. It also helps in recognizing faces and sounds.

Occipital Lobe

The occipital lobe controls vision. It processes visual information received from the eyes.

Cerebellum

The cerebellum controls balance, coordination, posture, and fine motor movements like writing.

Brain Stem

The brain stem controls automatic life functions such as breathing, heartbeat, and blood pressure.
